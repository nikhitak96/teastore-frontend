The history of tea leaf in the world

Tea has a long and fascinating history in China, dating back over 5,000 years. According to legend, Emperor Shen Nong discovered tea by accident and recognized its medicinal properties.

Tea didn't just stay in China - it spread to other parts of Asia too! In Japan, tea was brought over by Buddhist monks and developed into the tea ceremony, with matcha becoming popular.Different types of tea, such as green, black, and oolong, became popular over time.

In India, the British introduced tea, and chai, spiced tea with milk and sugar, became a staple.Sri Lanka has been producing tea for centuries, with Ceylon tea being famous worldwide. Each country has its own unique tea culture, but one thing is clear - tea is beloved around the world.
